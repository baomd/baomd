Neuromodulators in Aesthetic Dermatology

Dr. Quoc-Bao Nguyen • November 28, 2025 • 8 min read

Neuromodulators have become one of the most widely used tools in aesthetic medicine. Often reduced in popular culture to "wrinkle injections," their true value lies in controlled modulation of muscle activity—softening expression lines while preserving natural movement.

Used thoughtfully, neuromodulators enhance balance and expression. Used indiscriminately, they erase character.

How Neuromodulators Work

Neuromodulators temporarily block the release of acetylcholine at the neuromuscular junction, reducing muscle contraction. By weakening specific muscles, overlying skin relaxes and dynamic wrinkles soften.

Their effect is localized, dose-dependent, and reversible—features that make them powerful when guided by anatomy and restraint.

Common Uses

Neuromodulators are commonly used to treat:

  • Frown lines (glabella)
  • Forehead lines
  • Crow's feet
  • Excessive sweating (hyperhidrosis)

Beyond aesthetics, they play a role in medical dermatology for conditions involving muscle overactivity.

Product Differences Matter Less Than Technique

Multiple neuromodulator formulations are available. While there are pharmacologic differences, outcomes depend far more on:

  • Injection pattern
  • Dose selection
  • Understanding of facial dynamics
  • Individual muscle strength and asymmetry

Technique outweighs brand.

Preserving Expression

The face is dynamic. Complete muscle paralysis often produces an unnatural appearance and disrupts communication. Effective neuromodulator treatment aims to:

  • Soften lines, not eliminate movement
  • Maintain brow position and eye openness
  • Respect gender, age, and facial structure

Subtlety requires more planning than aggressiveness.

Longevity and Maintenance

Results typically last three to four months. Regular reassessment allows dosing and placement to evolve with facial changes over time.

Maintenance should be adaptive, not repetitive.

Safety and Expertise

Neuromodulators are safe when administered by trained professionals with detailed anatomical knowledge. Complications are uncommon but underscore the importance of:

  • Conservative dosing
  • Precise placement
  • Understanding diffusion patterns

Experience matters.

A Long-Term Perspective

Neuromodulators are most effective when integrated into a broader aesthetic plan that includes skin quality, volume support, and sun protection.

They are not a shortcut to youth, but a tool for preserving balance.

FDA-Approved Neuromodulators

  • Botox (onabotulinumtoxinA)
  • Dysport (abobotulinumtoxinA)
  • Xeomin (incobotulinumtoxinA)
  • Jeuveau (prabotulinumtoxinA)
  • Daxxify (daxibotulinumtoxinA)

Common Treatment Areas for Neuromodulators

  • Forehead lines
  • Glabellar frown lines
  • Crow's feet
  • Brow lift
  • Bunny lines
  • Lip flip
  • Masseter reduction
  • Hyperhidrosis (excessive sweating)
